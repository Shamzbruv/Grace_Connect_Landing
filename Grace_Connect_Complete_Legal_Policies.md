# Background Location Disclosure & Consent Text

### Required In-App Disclosure Before Permission Request
Grace Connect collects and uses your device location to enable automatic church attendance check-in when you are near your church during a scheduled service. If you enable automatic attendance, Grace Connect may use location data even when the app is closed or not actively in use, but only for attendance, geofencing, duplicate check-in prevention, late-status calculation, and attendance history. You can turn off automatic attendance or revoke location permission at any time in the app or your device settings.


### 1. Why Location Is Used
Location helps Grace Connect determine whether a user is physically near the church location during a scheduled service. This supports automatic attendance, manual check-in validation, duplicate prevention, late status, and accurate church records.


### 2. When Location May Be Collected
Location may be collected when a user opens the attendance feature, uses manual check-in, enables automatic attendance, or allows geofencing. If background location is enabled, the app may check location while the app is closed or not actively in use for the limited purpose of attendance detection.


### 3. What Data May Be Used
Grace Connect may use coordinates, permission status, distance from the church, church geofence radius, service schedule, arrival/dwell timing, check-in status, date, late status, and related attendance metadata.


### 4. What Location Is Not Used For
Grace Connect should not use location data for advertising, sale of data, unrelated tracking, profiling unrelated to church attendance, or sharing with unrelated third parties.


### 5. User Control
Users may disable automatic attendance inside the app where available, deny location permission, choose manual check-in where available, or revoke permission through Android or iOS device settings. Some attendance features may stop working if location is disabled.


### 6. Consent Checkbox Wording
“I understand that Grace Connect may use my device location, including background location if I enable automatic attendance, to support church attendance check-in and geofencing. I can turn this off at any time in the app or my device settings.”

# Security Incident & Data Breach Response Policy

### 1. Purpose
This policy guides response to suspected unauthorized access, data exposure, account compromise, storage misconfiguration, credential leak, malware, abusive admin activity, lost device, provider incident, or privacy breach.


### 2. Immediate Steps
1. Receive and log the report.
2. Classify severity and affected systems.
3. Contain the issue by disabling keys, accounts, roles, storage policies, or vulnerable features where necessary.
4. Preserve evidence and logs.
5. Investigate root cause and affected data.
6. Fix the issue and validate the fix.
7. Notify affected users, churches, service providers, regulators, or authorities where legally required.
8. Document lessons learned and prevention actions.


### 3. Breach Assessment
Grace Connect should assess what data was involved, whether sensitive data was exposed, how many users or churches were affected, whether the data was actually accessed, whether encryption or access controls reduced risk, and what legal notification duties apply.


### 4. Communications
Incident notices should be clear, factual, and timely. They should explain what happened, what data was involved, what action Grace Connect took, what users should do, and how to contact support.


### 5. Records
Incident records should include timestamps, affected systems, decision-makers, evidence, containment actions, communications, notifications, root cause, and remediation steps.
